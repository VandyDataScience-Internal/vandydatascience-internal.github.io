This year, we are considering applications for EMs, Developers, and Workshop Creators. EMs and Developers are project-facing positions, while Workshop Creators are not.

Our project-facing positions (EMs, Developers) will run throughout the duration of our project cycle (November through April). Projects are around a 2-4 hour/week time commitment for EMs and a 1-2 hour/week time commitment for Developers.

As an EM, you will be responsible for:

  • Creating the initial project description (including tech stack, GitHub etiquette, VSCode style guide, expected timeline, documentation guides, etc.)
  • Setting team expectations for the year
  • Delegating tasks to Developers based on their personal technical goals
  • Facilitating weekly coding sessions, check-ins, and standup meetings with Developers
  • Performing a monthly review of sprints (adding or changing the yearly expected timeline)
  • Designing the team's final poster presentation
  • Preparing a technical hand-over guide for future teams

As a Developer, you will be responsible for:

  • Understanding project specs and project etiquette outlined by your EM(s)
  • Checking in with your EM(s) about expectations for the year
  • Attending weekly coding sessions, check-ins, and stand-up meetings led by your EM(s)
  • Communicating with your EM(s) about progress and blockers

Our Workshop Creators will not be on project teams. Instead, each Workshop Creator will lead an hour-long workshop on a topic related to data science, artificial intelligence, or machine learning. These workshops will be open to the general body.

As a Workshop Creator, you will be responsible for:

  • Choosing a workshop topic
  • Familiarizing yourself on your chosen topic
  • Completing any necessary research
  • Creating a workshop lesson plan
